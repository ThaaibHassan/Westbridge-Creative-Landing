title: Restraint is a discipline

Every studio claims to care about craft. Fewer are willing to protect the empty space that craft needs.

Restraint is not austerity for its own sake. It is a decision to leave out anything that does not earn its place — a second typeface, a decorative scroll effect, a line of copy that says what the image already shows.

When we begin a project, we ask what the brand must carry, and what it can afford to set down. The answer is almost always smaller than the brief suggests.

That is how quiet work becomes memorable: not by being silent, but by being exact.
