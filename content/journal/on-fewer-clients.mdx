Westbridge is deliberately small. That is not a marketing posture — it is how the work gets done.

When a practice takes on too many concurrent projects, attention thins. Decisions get handed down rather than made. The artefact that ships is still competent, but something precise is lost between intent and execution.

We keep the roster short so the same people who shape the idea also see it through to launch. If that means saying no more often than yes, that is the trade we chose.
